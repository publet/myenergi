MyEnergi LifestyleTM

MyEnergi LifestyleTM shows how a typical American family can significantly reduce their electricity bills and CO2 footprint by integrating today’s smart home appliances, plug-in vehicles and solar energy. MyEnergi Lifestyle works by leveraging technology so key energy-consuming devices in a home use less energy, while also shifting energy usage to less expensive periods. At night, for example, when energy costs are lower, a smart refrigerator can perform high-energy tasks like ice making or defrosting – just as Ford’s plug-in vehicles do with recharging.

By switching to MyEnergi Lifestyle, families can cut energy costs by up to 60 percent and more than 9,000 kg of CO2 (a 55 percent reduction) saved. If every home in the U.S. were to implement these energy-saving technologies, it would be the equivalent of taking all the homes in California, New York and Texas – 32 million homes – off the power grid.

Collaborators

Key factors driving MyEnergi Lifestyle include:
  • Electric vehicles, like the all-new Ford C-MAX Energi plug-in hybrid, are more efficient and can charge during the low-cost electricity hours
  • Appliance efficiency has improved dramatically over the past decade
  • Hot water heaters are more efficient and use nighttime electricity
  • Connected appliances can use less energy during peak periods
  • Efficient, reliable and affordable rooftop solar systems enable families to generate renewable energy and save money on utility bills
  • Connected devices enable smarter use of energy
  • In addition to the energy benefits directly received from the latest product offerings from Ford, Eaton, SunPower and Whirlpool, the widespread deployment of smart meters (now in more than 40 million homes across the U.S.) enables households to take advantage of off-peak rates.

2013 MyEnergi Lifestyle Winners

For Susan Barry of Southern California, her experience on the Katie Couric show was one she'll never forget. Susan was the grand prize winner of a live drawing to receive a MyEnergi Lifestyle home makeover.

Half a year later the numbers are in to support the initiative’s claims that a properly-equipped home and strategic lifestyle choices can combine to help achieve a more cost-efficient, energy-efficient life. With her home outfitted with solar panels, energy-efficient appliances, and her new Ford C-MAX Energi Plug-In Hybrid, Susan has seen sharp decreases in her monthly utility bills. Her new refrigerator, for instance, cost $2.18 per week to run, nearly a dollar less than the cost of operating her old refrigerator. Small savings throughout her home added up to make a substantial dent in her monthly budget.

Solar power generated 85 percent of the electricity used in Susan's home. She also adopted smarter usage habits by charging her C-MAX Plug-In Hybrid between midnight and 3 a.m.-- non-peak hours that offer rates less than half of the daytime rates. To charge her vehicle’s battery from “empty” to “full” during non-peak time costs roughly 47 cents, compared to $1.23 during the day.

At the same time Susan won her home retrofit, Ford and the MyEnergi Lifestyle collaboration launched a nation-wide contest for one lucky family to also receive a MyEnergi Lifestyle home retrofit.

Ben and Lindsay Sattler of Colorado were chosen in July to be the next family to live an energy efficient life, courtesy of MyEnergi Lifestyle. In December, Ford and its collaborators completed the installation of solar panels and appliances in the Sattler home. The Sattlers’ home was retrofitted with solar panels and their appliances swapped for more energy-efficient models. They also turned in the keys of their 1996 Toyota Corolla in favor of a fuel-efficient Ford C-MAX Energi.

“Ben only has to fill up every two weeks now, whereas before he had to get gas every five to seven days,” Lindsay said. “Driving the C-MAX Energi has helped our pocketbooks immensely. Plus we absolutely love driving it!”
